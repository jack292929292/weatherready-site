Frequently Asked Questions

People plan ahead to reduce uncertainty and avoid problems. Many plans are made weeks or months in advance, so people naturally want to know what the weather might be like on a specific future date.

The problem, as we all know, is that weather forecasts become unreliable beyond 14 days. This leaves only two options: the long-term average or taking a guess. Both have major limitations. The average hides real variability, and a guess has no structure or reliability.

A valuable alternative would need to consistently outperform both of these options. That is the gap Weather Ready aims to fill.

Every estimate is displayed on the Estimation Accuracy plot once the target date has passed. How does Weather Ready differ from using the long-term average?

The long-term average cannot predict real variability for a specific future date. It hides hot, cold, wet, dry, and extreme outcomes. Weather Ready incorporates recent conditions and historical patterns to estimate where within that natural variability a specific date is more likely to fall, offering guidance the average cannot provide.

How does Weather Ready differ from simply guessing the weather?

A guess has no structure or connection to real atmospheric behaviour. Weather Ready builds a link between today‚Äôs conditions and the selected future date by simulating how temperatures typically evolve across the days in between. This structured, data-driven process is designed to consistently outperform random guessing.

To what extent can Weather Ready estimates be relied upon?

Weather Ready estimates should be used as helpful guidance, not as precise predictions. They provide a clearer expectation than using the long-term average or guessing, but all long-range forecasts carry uncertainty. The information can support early planning and risk assessment, but it should not be the sole basis for any important decision.
